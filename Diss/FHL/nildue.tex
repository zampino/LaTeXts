\section{definition of $\delta_{2}$}
Let $M$ be in $\nla{n}$, with $\cle$ we denote the $\Fp$-linear closure on the vector space $M_{1}$, while
$d_{1}$ will be the linear dimension on $M_{1}$. If $H=\gena{H_{1}}{M}$ is a subalgebra of $M$ we write $d_{1}(H)$ for $\dim_{\Fp}(H_{1})$.

\medskip
In what follows we concetrate on a subclass $\mathcal{K}_{2}$ of $\nla{2}$ and use a %$\delta_{2}$
positive $\cle$-predimension function to build
a limit via amalgamation of strong embeddings.
%Let $\fpe{M}$ denote the collection of finitely generated
%$\Fp$-vector subspaces of $M_{1}$. If $M\in\nla{2}$ and $H$ is a subalgebra of $M$, as usual we assume $H=\gena{H_{1}}{M}$ for some subspace $H_{1}$ of $M_{1}$, if moreover the latter belongs to $\fpe{M}$ then we will often and with abuse
%find $H\in\fpe{M}$.

If $A_1$ is a free $\Fp$-module with free basis $X$, then the free nilpotent algebra $\fla{2}{X}$ of $\nla{2}$, with generators from $X$, is isomorphic to
$$A_1\oplus\exs A_1.$$

For each $M\in\nla{2}$ %the quotient algebra $M\quot M^{3}$ is a $2$-nilpotent Lie algebra, as such a structure 
we can find an ideal of the exterior square $\exs M_{1}$ such that
$$M = M_{1}\oplus\Exs M_{1}\,\quot N^{2}(M).$$

If now $H=\gena{H_{1}}{M}$ is a subalgebra of $M$, identifying $\exs H_{1}$ with a \emph{natural} subspace
of $\exs M_{1}$ we define
$$N^{2}(H)=N^{2}(M)\cap\Exs H_{1}.$$

\medskip
Let $M\in\nla{2}$, we denote with $\fpe{M}$ the collection of all %subalgebras $H=\gena{H_{1}}{M}$ of $M$ such that $H_{1}$ is a finite dimensional subspace of $M$.
finite dimensional $\Fp$-subspaces of $M_1$. If $H_1\in\fpe{M}$, then with $H$ we will always denote the subalgebra $\gena{H_1}{M}$ of $M$.

Now we define $\map{\delta_{2}}{\fpe{M}}{\omega}$ as
\begin{labeq}{deltadue}
\delta_{2}(H_1)=d_{1}(H)-\dim_{\Fp}(N^{2}(H))\quad\forall H_1\in\fpe{M}.
\end{labeq}
Here is to be noted that the definition of $\delta_{2}$ depends only on the linear part $H_{1}$ of $H$.

\medskip
Our first issue is to show that $\delta_{2}$ is a $\cle$-predimension on $M_1$ for each $M\in\nla{2}$.
If $H$ and $K$ are in $\fpe{M}$ then with $H+K$ we denote the subalgebra $\gena{H_{1}+K_{1}}{M}$.

\bigskip
As in chapter one we obtain a dimensionfunction on $M_{1}$ if we set
$$d_{2}S=\delta_{2}^{*}\cle(S)=\delta_{2}^{*}{\gen{S}}_{1}\quad\forall S\in\fp{M_{1}}.$$

%Anyway, as we' re mostly working with linear objects, in this chapter the true arguments of the functions $\delta_{2}$ and $d_{2}$ will be only the vector subspaces of $M_{1}$.

\medskip
As before $H\in\fpe{M}$ will be a \emph{selfsufficient} or $2$-\emph{strong} substructure of $M$
if for all $C\in\fpe{M}$ such that $H\inn C$, we have $\delta_{2}C\geq\delta_{2}H$.

We write $H\zsu M$. As usual the same definition is applied to the underlying spaces of $M_{1}$, according
to this the expression $H_{1}\zsu M_{1}$ will also be found.

\section{Selfsufficient Closure}
Let $H\in\fpe{M}$, we define the \emph{selfsufficient closure} of $H$ as follows:
$$
\ssc_{2}H_{1}
=\!\bigcap_{\substack{H_{1}\inn C_{1}\zsu M_{1}\\C_{1}\in\fpe{M}}}\!\!C_{1}\quad\text{and}\quad\ssc_{2}(H)=\gena{\ssc_{2}(H_{1})}{M}
$$
So far, is $\ssc_{2}H$ the smallest $2$-strong substructure in $M$ containing $H$.

We will show that if $H\in\fpe{M}$ then $d_{2}H_{1}=\delta_{2}\ssc_{2}(H_{1})$.
%{\sl {\sl\bf vorsicht} was bedeutet $\ssc$ und wo ist's definiert!
%Kann man schreiben $\ssc(H)$ als $\gena{\ssc(H_{1})}{M}$ }

\medskip
From $d_{2}$ we also get a pregeometry  on $M_{1}$
given by the closure $\clz=\cl_{d_{2}}$ and a dimension notion
$\dim_{2}$ is introduced which will agree, on finite ($2$ dimensional) spaces with $d_{2}$.
Wir m\"ussen aber dabei achten, wir schreiben $\map{\clz}{\fpe{M}}{\fpe{M}}$ und wir meinen $\clz(H)=\gena
{\clz(H_{1})}{M}$ if $H=\gena{H_{1}}{M}$.

\medskip
Now we prove that $\ssc_{2}H\inn\clz H$. Assume $a$ is an element of $\ssc_{2}H_{1}$ then $d_{2}(Ha)=d_{2}H$, that's why
the family of $2$-strong spaces above $\gen{H_{1},a}_{1}$ coincides with that of $2$-strong substructures
above $H_{1}$.

In particular a $\clz$-space is selfsufficient.

\section{First Amalgam}
We define a class of %finitely generated
2-nilpotent Lie agebras that we want to be the \emph{Age} of our limit.
We require these algebras, not to have zero divisors.

Let $\Kdue=\{M\in\nla{2}\mid d_{1}(M)<\infty,\,\delta_{2}(H)\geq\min(2,d_{1}(H))\,\forall H\in\fpe{M}\}$.
%¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬
Now assume $A$ und $C$ are finitely generated algebras which have $B$ as common subalgebra.
Let $D_{1}=\vram{A_{1}}{B_{1}}{C_{1}}$ and consider the ideal $N^{2}(A)+N^{2}(C)$ of $\exs D_{1}$.
Now define the 2-algebra
$$\am{A}{B}{C}=D_{1}\oplus\exs D_{1}\quot(N^{2}(A)+N^{2}(C)).$$
We call $\am{A}{B}{C}$ the \emph{free amalgam} of $A$ and $C$ over $B$.
We aim at proving that if $A,B,C$ belong to $\Kdue$, then also $\am{A}{B}{C}$ does.

Assume $E\in\fpe{\am{A}{B}{C}}$, let $X^{B}$ be a basis of $B_{1}\cap E$, complete $X^{B}$ to a basis
of $A_{1}\cap E$ and $C_{1}\cap E$, with respectively $X^{B}X^{A}$ and $X^{B}X^{C}$, where
$X^{A}\inn A\non B$ and $X^{C}\inn C\non B$.

We have the following
\begin{lem}
We can choose vectors of $E$, $d_{1},\dots,d_{m}$ such that $d_{i}=u_{i}+v_{i}$, with
$\{u_{i}\}\inn A$ independent over $\gen{X^{B}X^{A}}$ and $\{u_{i}\}\inn C$ independent
over $\gen{X^{B}X^{C}}$ such that $E=\gen{X^{B}X^{A}X^{C},d_{1},\dots,d_{m}}$ and
$\mathcal{U}\mathcal{V}$ is idependent over $\gen{X^{B}X^{A}X^{C}}$.
\end{lem}

Next lemma provides a nice presentation of the elements from $N^{2}(E)$, for each $E$ in $\fpe{\am{A}{B}{C}}$.
\begin{lem}
Assume $E\in\fpe{\am{A}{B}{C}}$ and $\mathcal{B}=\{X^{B}<X^{A}<X^{C}<\mathcal{U}<\mathcal{V}$
is an ordered suitable basis. Then each element $\Phi$ of $N^{2}(E)$ is a sum of basic commutators
in $\mathcal{B}$, grouped as follows:
$$\Phi=\Phi^{}$$.
\end{lem}
