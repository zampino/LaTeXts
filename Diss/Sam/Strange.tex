Consider the following {\em free nil-$3$ algebras} extension $M=%\gen{M_{1}}\nni B=\gen{B_{1}}$ is %an extension of
\fla{3}{M_{1}}\nni B=\fla{3}{B_{1}}$ with $M_1=\gen{m^1,\,B_{1}}$ and $B_{1}=\gen{b^{1},b^{2},b^{3},b^{4}}$ (no relations at all).

\smallskip
Consider now $A_{1}=\gen{B_{1},a^{1},a^{2}}$ and a nil-$3$ algebra $A$ given by the presentation
$A=\gen{A_{1}\mid [b^{1},a^{1}]+[b^{2},b^{3}]+[b^{4},a^{2}]}$. %=\fla{3}{A_{1}}\quot(([b^{1},a^{1}]+[b^{2},b^{3}]+[b^{4},a^{2}]))$.
If we agree that $\gena{B_{1}}{A}\simeq\fla{3}{B_{1}}$ then we can proceed and say $M\dso B \dsu A$.
.

\smallskip
Choose basis $({\bf b}_{1}^{s}:=b^{s}| s=1,\dots,4)$ for $B_{1}$ and complete it with ${\bf m}_{1}^{1}:=m^{1}$
on one side so that $({\bf m}_{1}^{1},{\bf b}_{1}^{s})$ is a basis of $M_{1}$. On the other
hand set ${\bf a}_{1}^{t}:=a^{t}$ for $t=1,2$ so that $({\bf b}_{1}^{s},{\bf a}_{1}^{t})$ is a basis of $A_{1}$.
So $({\bf m}_{1}^{1},{\bf b}_{1}^{s},{\bf a}_{1}^{t})$ is a basis of $M_{1}\oplus_{B_{1}}A_{1}$.

\medskip
Define ${\bf b}_{2}^{1}:=\overline{[b^{2},b^{3}]}=[{\bf b}_{1}^{2},{\bf b}_{1}^{3}]\in B_{2}$ and complete it
arbitrarily to a basis $({\bf b}_{2}^{s})$
%\footnote{the representative of it in the quotient
%of $\fla{3}{A_{1}}$ modulo the relation $[b^{1},a^{1}]+[b^{2},b^{3}]+[b^{4},a^{2}]$.}
of $B_{2}$.

\smallskip
Set ${\bf m}_{2}^{r}:=[{\bf m}_{1}^{1},{\bf b}_{1}^{r}]$, so $({\bf m}_{2}^{r},{\bf b}_{2}^{s})$ is a basis of $M_{2}$.

\smallskip
Set ${\bf a}_{2}^{1}:=\overline{[b^{4},a^{2}]}=[{\bf b}_{1}^{4},{\bf a}_{1}^{2}]\in A_{2}\non B_{2}$ ({\sl is it? If not I can find a non trivial weight-$2$ relation based
over $B_{1}$}) and complete it arbitrarily to a basis $({\bf a}_{2}^{t})$ of $A_{2}$ over $B_{2}$.

\smallskip
Let now $({\bf b}_{3}^{s})$ be a basis of $B_{3}$ which is completed by means of
$({\bf m}_{3}^{r})$ and $({\bf a}_{3}^{t})$ to a basis of $M_{3}$ and of $A_{3}$ respectively.

\medskip
Now we define the {\em commutative product} $M\kp_{B}A$ as the nil-$3$ graded Lie algebra
with graded basis $\flat=({\bf m}^{r}_{i},{\bf b}_{i}^{s},{\bf a}_{i}^{t})_{i=1,2,3}$,
the same multiplication diagrams within $M$ and $A$ with respect of $\flat$ and with the extra conditions
$$[{\bf m}^{r}_{i},{\bf a}_{j}^{t}]=0$$
for all $i+j\leq3$ and all $r,t$.

\medskip
By this rule we have $0=[{\bf m}_{2}^{1},{\bf a}_{1}^{1}]=[[{\bf m}_{1}^{1},{\bf b}_{1}^{1}],{\bf a}_{1}^{1}]$ an  by Jacobi $[[{\bf m}_{1}^{1},{\bf b}_{1}^{1}],{\bf a}_{1}^{1}]=[[{\bf a}_{1}^{1},{\bf b}_{1}^{1}],{\bf m}_{1}^{1}]+[[{\bf m}_{1}^{1},{\bf a}_{1}^{1}],{\bf b}_{1}^{1}]$.

Since $[{\bf m}_{1}^{1},{\bf a}_{1}^{1}]=0$, we get $[[{\bf a}_{1}^{1},{\bf b}_{1}^{1}],{\bf m}_{1}^{1}]=0$ as well.

The defining relation of $A$ now yields $[{\bf a}_{1}^{1},{\bf b}_{1}^{1}]={\bf b}_{2}^{1}+{\bf a}_{2}^{1}$ and then
$[{\bf b}_{2}^{1},{\bf m}_{1}^{1}]+[{\bf a}_{2}^{1},{\bf m}_{1}^{1}]=0$. But this implies now
$[{\bf b}_{2}^{1},{\bf m}_{1}^{1}]=0$. This identity does not arise by a relation of $M$,
and hence $M$ is not a subalgebra of $M\kp_{B}A$.
